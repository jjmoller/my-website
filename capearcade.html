A scheduling app for a retro arcade in Cape Town

  • PROJECT: Cape Arcade
  • ROLE: Lead UX Designer
  • DURATION: 3 Months

Project Background

The Challenge

A local arcade is in need of a novel app to assist with the scheduling and booking of games for both individuals and groups. Two core functions have been identified: (1) Previewing available games in a digital catalogue and (2) making private bookings for both individuals and groups. Â 

With future updates, the app might include features such as an open forum for community discussions, mini-games created by the community, social awareness campaigns, and an online merch store.

The Vision

The successful design of a intuitive mobile app that will promote Cape Arcade's brand, make bookings easier for customers, provide access to current gaming news, and increase awareness around responsible gaming through social awareness campaigns.

The Process

The project was kicked off with the empathize stage of the Design Thinking process. This stage consisted of considerable user research which was then aggregated into user personas, problem statements, empathy maps, and user journey maps.

The user group confirmed many assumptions but other ideas and perspectives also surfaced throughout the project. Users are not only looking for entertainment, but also a easy way to socialise and connect with friends and family in a safe and fun environment.

User testing & Insight generation

Unmoderated user testing performed to identify and address any potential user pain points or missing features. Users from different socio-economical backgrounds were approached to participate in the user testing process. Listed below are a few of the insights that were gathered after identifying common themes expressed by users during testing.

1. Improved navigation

During the first round of usability tests, a number of users expressed that navigating only from the 'burger' menu was not intuitive. Users wanted a quicker way to navigate to their profile or their past bookings. This pain point was addressed by adding a navigation bar at the bottom of the home screen which allows for quick navigation to the settings/ profile screen, the new booking screen, and the past bookings screen.

During the second round of usability tests, users did not express any difficulty surrounding navigation from the home screen.
