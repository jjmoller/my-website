Project Background

The Challenge

To design a website centered around the short-term property rental market in South Africa. The website layout should be able to respond effectively so that users can make use of the plaform on a variety of devices such as desktops, laptops and smartphones.

Additionally, to promote accessibility within the design, the website should adhere to web accessibly standards as set out in the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Furthermore, a style guide should be defined early on to assist with the rapid design of both early concepts and final prototypes.

The Vision

The successful design of a intuitive property renting website which scales well and addresses the needs of its users - all enabled by great UI & UX design.

The Process

The project was kicked off with the empathize stage of the Design Thinking process. This stage consisted of considerable user research which was then aggregated into user personas, problem statements, empathy maps, and user journey maps.

User Empathy Map

Meet the Personas

Primary

James (31)

Accountant

James is a working professional who wants to rent a over the short term because he is leaving the country for another opportunity in six months time.
‍

‍Goals:
- To find a short-term rental on a platform that is easy to use and navigate.
- To be able to easily inquire on the availability of a listing with the property owner.

Frustrations/ Pain points:
James is frustrated by the fact that current property websites are saturated by property agents, and not listed by owners.

Secondary

Tammy (22)

Student

Tammy is a post-graduate student currently working hard on her thesis. She has been living at home, far away from campus but wishes to live closer to her academic support network during the final few months of university life.

Goals:
- To find a short term rental on campus.
- To have the option to share costs with a roommate.

Frustrations/ Pain points:
Tammy does not use social media and therefore feels that she misses out on short term rentals that are often posted on platforms such as Facebook and Instagram.

User Problem Statements

James

James is a skilled professional who needs to find a short term rental so that he can sell his house and not stress about signing a long-term rental contract.

Tammy

Tammy wants to move closer to her university so that she can be closer to her academic support network so that she can finish her studies on-time without having to stress about travelling to campus.
